memories with my beloved mother

why did you leave us so soon?

i had a lot of affection to my mom, when i heard of her demise, i was dumbfounded. why would God take her that faster? i wonder. I had prayed to him, pleaded him not to take her life away- since she is all i got. But I was wrong! He does things according to what he deems fit, not knowing the pain others will be left with.

mom we never celebrated your 50th birthday

i have lots of memories as follows:

  1. pre-primary level
  2. primary level
  3. high school level
  4. tertiary level
  5. at home
but why did you leave me and we had promised each other to wait until i finished my campus education, secure a job, then make you live like a queen? i had good news to share with you but.....

pre-primary memories

  • i liked school, and you were happy for me, i requested to join as soon as i turned five
  • you told me that i had relieved you a burden for you used to carry me to work- picking tea
  • i never let you down, since i would cry that i want to accompany my sibblings to school(they used to go to school at 6:00am)- i saved you time for arriving at the workplace. i never ever liked your job. i used to wish that i would grow up faster and make you stop plucking tea--but you disappointed me!!
  • sometimes my bro and i would be reluctant to go to school-a behaviour i never knew where i had developed it from. you would give us a push, with beatings up to pyrethrum

primary school..

you being away from us for a couple of years

you conflicted with dad when i was in class one. You parted ways, and i lived with dad up to when i was in class four, heading five- when francis was getting circumcised. But i always felt your presence even from now henceforth i believe you will never leave me... not even for a second.

being the best in class

you were happy for me when i would defeat others in the school examnations. not once or twice did you come for prize-giving ceremonies, and you would proudly step forward to accompany me when receiving my gift,,i was ever in the top of the list.

somehow being notorious

There was a time when i was in grade four, you were called by my class teacher, madam Esther, because i had abused other pupils- by beating them. But to my amazement, you never punished me, that made me regret and i stopped that habit

High school life

  • on 1st January 2019, you broke the news to me that you never had enough money to take me to a boarding school. You pleaded me to go to Mukuyu on condition that i would never get returned home due to school fees
  • i never let you down, and worked hard, i remember when i scored a 99% in history test! you were extremely happy and congratulated me. Unfortunately i scored a C+ in KCSE which was a great shock to me. You mourned with me-thank you
  • When i told you that i wanted to repeat, since i was not satisfied with that grade, you never turned me down. You took a loan and paid fees and exam in Bathi High, and responded on time when i called due to a problem
  • How happy i was when you came on prayers day!!- i never told you. I felt special

campus life..

  • i never had an opportunity to share with you the campus experience
  • eventhough you had insufficient money, i insisted on reporting, if bad bad,, you send me to the posho mill to mill my maize into maize flour
  • in the morning you gave me a hundred bob as pocket money...i said nothing since i knew your situation
  • also in that morning i was leaving, you woke up irregardless of you feeling unwell. you went to the shamba and packed for me sukuma, of which i ate for three days.
  • i longed to narrate to you the tiring registration process. When i calle you requestin for 500 shillings for x-ray medical report, i knew you had no money, but i believed yow would get me. you sent David and after some minutesm i was attended to.
  • in that same afternoon, i called you and told you that the university had declined admitting me without paying a little fees. i knew i was stressing you out but i had no otherwise
  • you spoke to Njoki, who sent me 2400, which i paid and got admitted

At home

You used to tell me that sometimes i talked too much,, but i always wanted to break the boredom and make you smile. tou told me that when i was an infant, i talked first, before i could even walk. You told me that you used to be happy since we would converse eventhough i was little!

mom you only punished me once. You were very cool. But that was your strength since i never wanted to wrong you.

sometimes i would get so moody and you never talked to me at that moment. You always waited for me to cool down my tempers
